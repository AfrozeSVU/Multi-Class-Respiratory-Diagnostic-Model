Root Cause — Domain Shift:
  - Training data (ICBHI): clinical-grade stethoscope recordings, controlled environment
  - External data (Kaggle): different recording devices, environments, patient demographics
  - Bronchiectasis recall collapses from 95% → 8% (most domain-sensitive class)
  - COPD recall stays high at 96% (dominant class, most robust learned features)

5. RESUME / IMPACT SUMMARY (XYZ Format)

BULLET 1 — Core Achievement:
  Built a 5-class respiratory disease classifier using transfer learning on ResNet-18,
  achieving 94.5% accuracy and 0.945 macro F1-score on the ICBHI 2017 benchmark dataset
  across COPD, Pneumonia, Bronchiectasis, Healthy, and URTI classes.

BULLET 2 — Data Engineering:
  Engineered a full audio preprocessing pipeline (MFCC extraction at 44.1kHz, 40 coefficients,
  680 time frames) and resolved a 49.5x class imbalance (792 COPD vs 16 Bronchiectasis samples)
  using cap-count augmentation (time-stretch, pitch-shift, noise injection), balancing all
  5 classes to ~200 samples each.

BULLET 3 — Generalization / Honest Result:
  Validated model on 1,211 unseen samples from an independent Kaggle dataset, achieving
  47.9% cross-dataset accuracy — identifying domain shift as the primary failure mode
  (Bronchiectasis recall dropped from 95% → 8% due to recording condition mismatch),
  demonstrating rigorous evaluation methodology beyond internal benchmarks.

BULLET 4 — Engineering Decisions:
  Implemented weighted CrossEntropyLoss, ReduceLROnPlateau scheduler, early stopping
  (patience=8), and aggressive dropout (0.7) to close a train/val loss gap, improving
  validation F1 by closing overfitting on a small medical audio dataset (~1,000 samples).
